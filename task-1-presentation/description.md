# Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) — free browser-based design tool

## What the Design Shows

The infographic is titled **"Be Digitally Literate: Your Guide to the Online World"** and is divided into three clearly labelled sections:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?**  
   A brief definition explaining that digital literacy means the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies responsibly and effectively.

2. **Safe Internet Practices**  
   Key safety tips illustrated with icons: use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, avoid clicking unknown links, protect personal data, and report suspicious activity.

3. **Professional Online Presence**  
   Tips on building a credible online profile: keep profiles updated, use a professional photo, avoid posting sensitive personal information, and engage respectfully on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.

## Design Details
- Colour palette: Blue and white with orange accents
- Layout: Three-column grid with icons and short text blocks
- Font: Poppins (clean and modern)
- Exported as a PNG and added to this folder

## Reflection
Using Canva was straightforward thanks to its drag-and-drop interface and ready-made templates. The most interesting part was selecting icons that matched each concept without cluttering the layout. The most difficult part was keeping text concise — fitting meaningful information into small boxes required rewording sentences multiple times. I also discovered that choosing a consistent colour palette early saves a lot of editing time later.
