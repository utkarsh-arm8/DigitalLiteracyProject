# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Crime Type
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) / Online Payment Fraud

---

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorising money transfers or revealing sensitive credentials through psychological manipulation, fake apps, or social engineering tactics. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI fraud exploits the speed and convenience of instant digital payments — and the victim often realises too late.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step by Step)

1. **Target identification:** The fraudster identifies a target — often someone who has recently listed a product for sale on OLX, Quikr, or a local Facebook group.
2. **Contact:** The fraudster calls or messages, pretending to be a genuine buyer or a bank customer care executive.
3. **Creating urgency:** They claim they want to pay immediately via UPI and ask the victim to "receive" money.
4. **Sending a fake collect request:** Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a UPI *collect request* — which *debits* the victim's account when approved.
5. **Victim approves the request:** Believing they are accepting a payment, the victim enters their UPI PIN, which authorises the debit.
6. **Money is stolen:** The amount is instantly transferred out, often to a mule account abroad.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students and young adults selling second-hand items online
- First-time UPI users unfamiliar with the collect vs. send distinction
- Small business owners and daily wage workers
- Elderly individuals not well-versed in digital payment flows

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often ₹5,000–₹50,000 in student cases)
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty recovering funds due to the irreversible nature of UPI transactions
- In severe cases, victims take on debt to recover losses
