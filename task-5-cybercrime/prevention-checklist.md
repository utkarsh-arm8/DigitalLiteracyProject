# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students (India)

## 🔒 General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Combine uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden — free) rather than reusing passwords.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorised access.

- [ ] **3. Never click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email. Fraudsters create fake websites that look identical to real banks or government portals.

- [ ] **4. Verify the identity of anyone who contacts you** claiming to be a bank representative, delivery executive, or government official. Real banks never ask for your OTP, PIN, or CVV over call or message.

- [ ] **5. Keep your device software updated.** OS and app updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit.

- [ ] **6. Use trusted Wi-Fi networks only.** Avoid accessing banking apps or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi (cafes, airports, hostels) without a VPN.

---

## 💰 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. Understand the difference between "Send" and "Collect" on UPI.** Entering your PIN on a collect request sends money OUT of your account — it does NOT receive money. Never approve a collect request from an unknown person.

- [ ] **8. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account number with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay, PhonePe, or NPCI. No legitimate service ever asks for this.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Communication Safety

- [ ] **9. Audit your social media privacy settings** every six months. Limit who can see your posts, phone number, and friend list.

- [ ] **10. Do not respond to messages asking you to forward a number or code** sent to your phone. This is a common WhatsApp account hijacking method.

---

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Helpline** | Call **1930** (24×7, pan-India) |
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | File a complaint at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Your Bank** | Call the bank's helpline immediately and request a transaction hold |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at the nearest cyber cell |

> ⚡ **Act within the first hour.** The faster you report a UPI or banking fraud, the higher the chance of a reversal or account freeze.
