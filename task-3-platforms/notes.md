## Part B — Google Workspace: Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz

**Tool used:** Google Forms (forms.google.com)

I created a 5-question **"Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz"** designed for first-year students.

### Questions in the Form:
1. What does the term "digital literacy" mean? *(Multiple Choice)*
2. Which of the following is a safe internet practice? *(Multiple Choice)*
3. What should you do if you receive a suspicious email asking for your password? *(Multiple Choice)*
4. Name one platform you would use to showcase your coding projects online. *(Short Answer)*
5. In your own words, what is the biggest online risk you think students face today? *(Short Answer)*

The form was linked to a Google Sheet to automatically collect and display responses.

## Reflection

HackerRank gave me a structured environment to write and test code without needing any local setup — just a browser. Even basic challenges helped me understand how competitive programming judges work (input → output → test cases). Google Forms turned out to be more powerful than I expected; the automatic response collection via Google Sheets means I could actually use this quiz in my Digital Ambassador role to collect real data from batchmates. These tools will help me academically by lowering the barrier to practising code and collaborating on group tasks without email attachments.
