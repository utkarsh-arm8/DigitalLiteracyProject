# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings.** Regularly review and update who can see your posts, friend list, and personal information. |
| 4 | **Credit others' work.** If you share someone else's content, always tag or mention the original creator. |
| 5 | **Engage respectfully.** Disagree politely in comments, support peers publicly, and contribute meaningfully to discussions. |
| 6 | **Keep personal details private.** Avoid sharing your phone number, home address, or daily routine publicly. |
| 7 | **Use separate accounts if needed.** Maintain one professional account (LinkedIn, GitHub) and one personal account, and keep standards high on both. |

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't post during exams or crises.** Avoid sharing complaints about professors, classmates, or the institution publicly. |
| 2 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers.** Unknown accounts may be fake profiles used for phishing or social engineering. |
| 3 | **Don't share unverified news or forwards.** Always check a source before resharing — misinformation spreads quickly and reflects on you. |
| 4 | **Don't overshare location data.** Avoid tagging your real-time location or checking in at places habitually. |
| 5 | **Don't engage with trolls or cyberbullies.** Block, report, and move on. Responding escalates the situation and harms your wellbeing. |
| 6 | **Don't use social media to vent academic frustration.** Public complaints can damage relationships with faculty and your institution. |
| 7 | **Don't share copyrighted material without permission.** This includes music, films, images, and academic content. |
